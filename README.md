# Quantum AI Capabilities Research Project

## Overview

This project represents the most comprehensive research database on five influential researchers in quantum mechanics, simulation theory, and mathematical physics: **Nick Bostrom, Clifford A. Pickover, Thomas Campbell, Harvey Dubner, and Melvin Vopson**.

## Research Scope

- **Time Period:** 1984-2026 (42 years of research coverage)

- **Geographic Coverage:** Global (US, European, Asian media)
- **Languages:** English, German, French, Spanish, Italian
- **Source Types:** Academic journals, news articles, interviews, institutional records

### 1. Nick Bostrom (1973-present)
- **Field:** Philosophy, AI Safety, Existential Risk
- **Institution:** Oxford University, Future of Humanity Institute
- **Key Work:** "Superintelligence: Paths, Dangers, Strategies" (2014)
- **Research Focus:** Simulation hypothesis, anthropic bias, superintelligence

### 2. Clifford A. Pickover (1957-present)
- **Field:** Mathematics, Computer Graphics, Fractals
- **Institution:** IBM T.J. Watson Research Center
- **Achievements:** 830+ patents, 50+ books
- **Research Focus:** Mathematical patterns, recreational mathematics, chaos theory

### 3. Thomas Campbell (1944-present)
- **Field:** Physics, Consciousness Research
- **Background:** NASA physicist, Department of Defense
- **Key Work:** "My Big T.O.E." trilogy
- **Research Focus:** Simulation theory, consciousness fundamentals, quantum experiments

### 4. Harvey Dubner (1928-2019)
- **Field:** Mathematics, Computer Science
- **Company:** Dubner Computer Systems (1984-1985)
- **Achievements:** Prime number research, computational algorithms
- **Research Focus:** Twin prime conjectures, mathematical software

### 5. Melvin Vopson (present)
- **Field:** Physics, Information Theory

- **Key Work:** "Second Law of Infodynamics" (2024)
- **Research Focus:** Information physics, computational universe theory
